The course aims to develop the skills of the students in the areas of boundary value problems and transform techniques. This will be necessary for their effective studies in a large number of engineering subjects like heat conduction, communication systems, electro-optics and electromagnetic theory. The course will also serve as a prerequisite for post graduate and specialized studies and research.

Engineering mathematics is the art of applying maths to complex real-world problems; combining mathematical theory, practical engineering and scientific computing to address today's technological challenges.

Topics

  • UNIT I : Linear Differential Equations (LDE) and Applications
  • UNIT II : Transforms
  • UNIT III : Statistics
  • UNIT IV : Probability and Probability Distributions
  • UNIT V : Vector Calculus
  • Unit VI : Applications of Partial Differential Equations (PDE)
